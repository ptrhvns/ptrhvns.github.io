Imposter Syndrome

⇐ Home

I figured out a way to work around the times when I'm suffering from imposter syndrome. First, I accept any of the bad thoughts I have about myself as being true. Then, importantly, I get started anyway.

The way I like to get started is to ask, "How could someone who's [bad thought] do [task] or achieve [goal]?" That question changes my focus from perceived deficiencies to action. For example, if I were feeling bad at visual design, and that was keeping me from building a website, I would say to myself, "How could someone who's bad at visual design build a website?"

As long as I actually notice I'm suffering from imposter syndrome, and I remember to use this trick, it almost always makes things better. The negative energy just drains right out.

I think the reason is that I don't have to look like the genius who figured it all out, and I don't have to feel bad when I don't. My identity isn't tied to who I am. Instead, it's tied to what I'm doing. That only requires that I keep showing up. No skill or talent are necessary. If I keep making an effort, eventually something good will happen.
