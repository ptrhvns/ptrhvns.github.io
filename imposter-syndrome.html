Imposter Syndrome

⇐ Home

May 5th, 2021

I figured out a way to work around the times when I'm suffering from imposter syndrome. First, I accept any negative thoughts I have about myself as being true. Then, I get to work on whatever task or goal I was blocked on anyway.

I usually kick things off by asking, "How could [negative thought] finish [blocked task], or achieve [blocked goal]?" For example, if I were feeling bad at design, and that was keeping me from building a website, I would say to myself, "How could someone who's bad at design build a nice website?"

That question changes my focus from my own deficiencies to getting results. As long as I notice I'm having a problem, and I remember to use this trick, it almost always solves the problem.

I don't have to look like the genius who figured it all out, and I don't have to feel bad when I don't. My identity isn't tied to how amazing I am. Instead it's tied to putting in the effort. I can do that. I know I can keep trying. That only requires that I keep showing up. No skill or talent is necessary.

It takes the pressure off of having to be perfect. I can fail, because "I'm just throwing mud against the wall until something sticks." It makes it easy to trust in myself, and know that I'll figure something out, especially if I keep my current task or goal simple. Then, I can just iterate to accomplish something big.
